Performance Indicators — PHE Canada

The 5 Teaching Cues

"Look Ahead"
Eyes remain focused forward throughout the entire run — not down at the feet
"High Knees"
Knees bend at right angles during the recovery phase of the stride
"Pump Arms"
Arms bend at elbows and move in opposition to the legs — left arm / right leg
"Light Feet"
Front part of foot contacts the ground first — not flat-footed heel strike
"Lean Forward"
Body leans slightly forward — momentum-driven posture, not upright or backward
Stage 1 of 4

Initial Stage: Run High Guard

❌ Arms — High Guard
Arms held high and swing outward to the sides. No cross-body opposition. Hands near shoulder height.
❌ Flat-Footed Contact
Full foot contacts the ground simultaneously. No forefoot strike. Short, wide stride pattern.
❌ Wide Shoulder Stance
Legs swing out wide — shoulder-width or wider. Body feels unstable so it compensates with width.
❌ No Flight Phase
One foot always contacts ground. No true running yet — both feet never off ground at same time.
Initial Stage K–1 typical
Stage 2 of 4

Elementary A: Middle Guard

🔄 Arms — Middle Guard
Arms drop to mid position. Cross-body movement starts to emerge but remains inconsistent.
↕️ Vertical Bounce Still High
Much energy wasted going up and down. Stride lengthens but remains bouncy and inefficient.
📏 Stride Increases
A brief flight phase begins to appear — both feet briefly off the ground. True running starts here.
🔄 Arm Swing Increases
Arm swing grows but still inconsistent. Elbow bend approaching 90° but still crosses midline at times.
Elementary A Grades 1–3
Stage 3 of 4

Elementary B: Heel-Toe Arms Extended

🔄 Arms — Low Guard
Arms drop further. Opposition improves significantly. Elbows nearly fully extended during arm swing.
🦶 Heel-Toe Contact
Foot strike transitions toward heel-toe pattern — a key transitional marker of improved mechanics.
🔀 Opposition Emerging
Left arm / right leg opposition becomes visible and more consistent. Rotational forces begin helping propulsion.
📐 Less Vertical Bounce
Energy increasingly directed forward. Running becomes noticeably more efficient and purposeful.
Elementary B Grades 2–4
Stage 4 of 4 — The Goal

Mature Stage: Pumping Arms

✅ Toe-Heel Contact (Sprinting)
Forefoot lands first. Efficient energy transfer from ground to forward propulsion. No wasted impact.
✅ Full Arm-Leg Opposition
Left arm drives forward with right leg perfectly synchronized. Trunk rotation adds power to each stride.
✅ High Heel Recovery
Back foot kicks up high toward the glutes during recovery — compact and mechanically efficient stride cycle.
✅ Elbow Flexion ~90°
Arms bent at right angles. Drive forward and back — not across the body. Relaxed hands, no tight fists.
Mature Stage Grades 3–5 goal

Difficulties to Watch For — PHE Canada

Common Errors & Fixes

If you see this…Then try this…
Small steps and little flight Show them how to exaggerate stride length — "giant steps" between cones
Feet spread too wide apart Coach them to run along a painted line, keeping feet directly over it
Short strides Place beanbags on a line — students must widen stride to step over each one
Low heel recovery Short sprint with heels kicking up to touch their bottom on each stride
Arms swinging side to side Stand still and only move arms — they should not cross midline; hands lightly brush hips
Leaning too far forward Run on toes, stay as tall as possible — "run like you're proud"
Assessment Protocol — TGMD-2

How We Assess Running

"Run as fast as possible between 2 cones set 60 feet apart."
TGMD-2 Assessment Protocol
📋 What to observe
All 5 cues: eye direction, arm bend and opposition, forefoot strike, body lean, and knee bend at 90°.
🔁 Two trials
Give two trials per student. Take the best performance observed. Assess at start and end of unit.
Checklist
Eyes focused forward "Look Ahead"
Knees at 90° recovery "High Knees"
Arm opposition to legs "Pump Arms"
Forefoot ground contact "Light Feet"
Slight forward lean "Lean Forward"
Assessment Activity

The Locomotor License Test

Students "drive" around the gym. Running = the interstate at speed limit 55. Those who maintain all 5 cues earn their Locomotor License.

Summary

Key Takeaways

🔢
4 Stages of Development
High Guard → Middle Guard → Heel-Toe Arms Extended → Pumping Arms. Each has specific visible indicators.
📣
5 Teaching Cues
"Look Ahead" · "High Knees" · "Pump Arms" · "Light Feet" · "Lean Forward" — short, memorable, actionable.
🔧
6 Common Errors
Every error has a specific fix. Observe → name the error → apply the targeted strategy. Don't just repeat the cue louder.
📏
60-Foot Assessment
TGMD-2: two cones, 60 feet, run as fast as possible. Assess all 5 performance indicators per student.
🏆
Pre-Control → Control → Utilization → Proficiency
Running underpins every sport and game. Invest in this skill and students unlock everything else in their physical education journey.
