OUR APPROACH

Every child develops at their own pace. We meet students where they are and build from there.

Movement is a language.
We teach every child to speak it fluently.

In Coach Hin's PE class, we follow a research-based framework for Fundamental Movement Skills (FMS). Students progress through developmental stages — Initial, Elementary, and Mature — building competence and confidence along the way.

Our curriculum covers three skill families: Locomotor (moving the body through space), Manipulative (controlling objects), and Balancing (controlling the body itself).

Parents and students can use this page to understand expectations, recognize teaching cues used in class, and practice at home. Everything here connects directly to what happens in the gym.

Category One

LOCOMOTOR SKILLS

Moving the body through space. Six foundational skills every K–5 student develops.

Locomotor · 4 Stages
RUNNING
Teaching Cues
  • "Look Ahead" Eyes focused forward throughout
  • "High Knees" Knees bend at right angles
  • "Pump Arms" Arms move in opposition to legs
  • "Light Feet" Front of foot contacts ground
  • "Lean Forward" Body leans slightly forward
Assessment
Run as fast as possible between 2 cones set 60 feet apart
Locomotor · 3 Stages
GALLOP / SLIDE
Teaching Cues
  • "Trail Leg" Keep trail leg behind lead leg
  • "Lead Leg" Can gallop using either leg
  • "Arms With Beat" Rhythmical arm movement
  • "Twist to Direction" Hips, shoulders, eyes all aligned
Assessment
Gallop from one cone to the other for about 25 feet
Locomotor · 4 Stages
HOPPING
Teaching Cues
  • "One Foot" Take off and land same foot
  • "Make an L Shape" Knee at 90° behind body
  • "Arms" Use arms for lift
  • "Hop to a Beat" Rhythmical hopping pattern
  • "Controlled" Maintain balance throughout
Assessment
Hop 3 times on one foot then the other foot
Locomotor · 4 Stages
HORIZ. JUMP
Teaching Cues
  • "Look Ahead" Eyes focused forward
  • "Bend Then Explode" Prep: bend knees and waist
  • "Swing" Full arms swing back-to-forward
  • "Expand" Extend body in flight with bent knees
  • "Soft Feet" Take off and land on two feet
Assessment
Jump as far as possible from a starting line on the floor
Locomotor · 4 Stages
LEAPING
Teaching Cues
  • "Move Forward" Sustained forward movement in leap
  • "Looking Ahead" Eyes focused forward
  • "Opposite Feet" Take off one foot, land other
  • "Straight Legs" Legs extended in flight
  • "Control" Controlled landing, no balance loss
Assessment
Run then leap over a beanbag starting from tape
Locomotor · 3 Stages
SKIPPING
Teaching Cues
  • "Step-Hop" Displays a step-hop pattern
  • "Left Then Right" Uses both sides alternately
  • "Arm Swing" Arms swing in opposition to feet
  • "Keep a Beat" Displays rhythmical pattern
  • "Look Ahead" Eyes focused forward
Assessment
Skip from one cone to another for about 25 feet
03
Category Two

MANIPULATIVE SKILLS

Controlling and interacting with objects using different body parts and implements.

Manipulative · 5 Stages
CATCHING
Teaching Cues
  • "Look" Eyes on object throughout entire catch
  • "Get Behind" Position body in path of object
  • "Ready" One foot slightly ahead, balanced
  • "Pinkies / Thumbs" Correct hand orientation
  • "Soft Hands" Relax and absorb force of object
Assessment
4' ball, 2 lines 15ft apart — toss underhand to child aiming at chest
Manipulative · 4 Stages
DRIBBLING
Teaching Cues
  • "Look Ahead" Eyes forward, not on ball
  • "5 Fingers" Fingertip contact with ball
  • "Yo-Yo" Bend and straighten wrist/elbow
  • "Bend & Lean" Hips and knees slightly flexed
  • "Opp. Foot Fwd" Dribble in front, to the side
Assessment
Dribble ball 4x without moving; repeat on other side
Manipulative · 4 Stages
KICKING
Teaching Cues
  • "Eyes on Target" Focus on the target
  • "Plant Foot" Step beside ball with non-kicking foot
  • "Lean In" Bend at waist, initiate kick from hip
  • "Shoelaces" Contact ball with instep
  • "Swing Through" Follow through toward target
Assessment
Run and kick a stationary ball for 20 feet
Manipulative · 5 Stages
OVERHAND THROW
Teaching Cues
  • "Eyes on Target" Focus on target throughout
  • "W" Elbow back at shoulder height, behind ear
  • "Rotate" Hips rotate, opposite shoulder to target
  • "Step Forward" Opposite foot steps forward
  • "Follow Through" Lead with elbow, follow down across
Assessment
20 ft from wall — throw tennis ball hard at the wall
Manipulative · 4 Stages
STRIKING
Teaching Cues
  • "Watch Ball" Eyes on object being struck
  • "Stand Sideways" Side on to target
  • "Swing Back" Preparatory backswing displayed
  • "Rotate" Hip and shoulder rotation during swing
  • "Follow Through" Follow through along swinging path
Assessment
Hit 4' ball off batting tee — hit hard
Manipulative · 3 Stages
UH ROLLING
Teaching Cues
  • "Eyes on Target" Focus eyes on the target
  • "Swing" Backward and forward arm swing
  • "3 Steps" Step forward with opposite leg
  • "Bend & Release Low" Bend knees, release along floor
  • "Follow Through" Hand pointing at target after release
Assessment
Roll from 20 ft away between 2 cones set 4 ft apart

Category Three

BALANCING

Controlling the body — both still and in motion. Static and dynamic balance both matter.

Balance · 3 Stages
STATIC
Holding still while maintaining balance
Teaching Cues
  • "Control" Maintains a steady balance
  • "Correct" Adjusts body to maintain balance
  • "Looking Forward" Eyes focused forward at all times
Developmental Stages
Initial
Very unstable; raises non-supporting leg several inches; overcompensates with outside support
Elementary
Performs better on dominant leg; uses arms for balance; cannot balance with eyes closed
Mature
Can balance with eyes closed; uses arms and trunk as needed; changes to non-dominant leg
Assessment
Stork stance on one leg then the other — hold for 30 seconds
Balance · 3 Stages
DYNAMIC
Maintaining balance while moving through space
Teaching Cues
  • "Movement" Maintains balance while alternate stepping
  • "Good Posture" Maintains an upright posture
  • "Control" Maintains balance by using arms as needed
  • "Looking Forward" Eyes focused forward at all times
Developmental Stages
Initial
Balances with support; eyes on feet; uses follow step with dominant foot lead
Elementary
Eyes focused on beam; loses balance easily; uses alternate stepping action
Mature
Movements fluid, relaxed, and in control; eyes focused beyond beam; loses balance rarely
Assessment
Walk across a balance beam forwards
